Beverage Trends in India:
The Shift Towards Health-Conscious Choices

April 2, 2026
8 min read
4.2k views
The evolving beverage aisle — functional drinks lead India's new health wave.

India's beverage market stands at a fascinating crossroads. For decades, the conversation was dominated by sugary carbonated drinks and mass-market teas. Today, a well-informed, health-aware consumer is reshaping every shelf, every menu, and every production line across the country. The shift is not subtle — it is seismic.

At Team24, we have had a front-row seat to this transformation. From our distribution network spanning over 14 states to our brand partnerships with emerging functional drink makers, the signals are unmistakable: Indians are drinking differently, and they are demanding more from the beverages they consume every day.

Key Takeaways

Functional beverages — drinks with added vitamins, electrolytes, or adaptogens — are the fastest-growing segment in India's FMCG landscape.
Post-pandemic health consciousness has permanently elevated consumer expectations around ingredients, sugar content, and sourcing transparency.
Regional flavours — kokum, aam panna, jal jeera — are finding new commercial relevance as premiumised RTD (Ready-to-Drink) products.
Sustainability in packaging is no longer optional — eco-friendly packaging now directly influences purchase decisions for 41% of urban buyers.

The Rise of Functional Beverages

The functional beverage segment — encompassing energy drinks, immunity boosters, gut health formulations, and adaptogen-infused waters — has seen an 18% year-on-year growth in India, according to category tracking data. This is not a trend confined to metro cities. Tier-2 and Tier-3 towns are increasingly part of this story, driven by rising disposable incomes and a youth demographic that actively seeks performance nutrition.

Brands like Paper Boat, RAW Pressery, and a new wave of D2C players have normalised the premium end of this spectrum. Consumers are reading labels. They are comparing sugar content. They are Googling what "ashwagandha extract" does before picking it off a shelf. The informed Indian consumer has arrived.

"The Indian consumer no longer buys a drink to quench thirst alone — they are buying a feeling, a function, and increasingly, a value system."

— Team24 Market Intelligence Report, 2026

Regional Flavours Find Premium Ground

One of the most exciting developments in Indian beverages is the resurgence of traditional regional flavours in modern, premium formats. Kokum sherbet from Goa and the Konkan coast, aam panna from Maharashtra, jal jeera from North India — these are not novelties. They are ancient recipes being repackaged for the contemporary consumer.

What makes this particularly powerful is the authenticity edge that local brands carry. When a Goan kokum drink is positioned as an antioxidant-rich, climate-appropriate refresher with zero artificial additives, it does not compete with cola — it operates in an entirely different aspirational space.

Traditional Indian ingredients are finding new life in modern beverage formats.

Team24's Strategic Response

For Team24, this shift represents both an opportunity and a responsibility. Our distribution infrastructure, built over years across India's diverse markets, positions us uniquely to accelerate promising new-age beverage brands into mainstream retail. But more than logistics, the opportunity is about curation.

We are actively working with brands that align with three non-negotiables: clean ingredient sourcing, responsible packaging, and honest marketing. The era of empty health claims on beverage labels is drawing to a close, and we believe that brands who build genuine credibility now will command disproportionate loyalty in the years ahead.

Sustainability as a Core Metric

No conversation about India's evolving beverage landscape is complete without addressing the sustainability imperative. The country generates enormous volumes of single-use plastic waste from beverage packaging, and regulatory pressure — alongside genuine consumer concern — is accelerating a shift toward recyclable, biodegradable, and refillable formats.

Team24 has committed to achieving net-zero packaging impact across our partner portfolio by 2030. This is not a marketing statement — it is a supply-chain commitment. We are actively piloting glass-return programmes in select markets and working with co-packers who have invested in lightweighting technology to reduce material use without compromising shelf integrity.

What Comes Next

The next three to five years in Indian beverages will be defined by a few macro forces: the continued premiumisation of everyday drinks, the maturation of D2C distribution as a viable long-term channel, and the potential for regulatory clarity around health claims that could reshape how brands communicate benefits.

Team24 is positioned at the intersection of all three. We see our role not just as distributors, but as category builders — helping brands navigate the complex, vast, and breathtakingly diverse Indian market with the agility, intelligence, and partnerships that create lasting value.

The Indian consumer is ready. The question is whether brands and distributors will rise to meet them.
